How I work

A short, repeatable approach I use to move ambiguous problems toward reliable outcomes.

  1. Investigate — start with facts

    I collect logs, reproduce the issue, and ask targeted questions to separate symptoms from root causes.

  2. Prioritize — keep impact first

    I weigh user impact, effort, and risk to pick the smallest meaningful action that reduces customer pain.

  3. Communicate — clarity at every step

    I surface trade-offs, document decisions, and give clear next steps to teams and customers.

  4. Document — make knowledge reusable

    I capture diagnostic steps, runbooks, and FAQ entries so fixes are repeatable and onboarding is faster.

  5. Improve — measure and iterate

    I track metrics, remove manual steps, and automate small, high-value tasks over time.

Case studies

Three concise examples that show how I break down problems and deliver results. Expand each item to read the structure: Problem • Constraints • Action • Outcome.

Payment failures after a release — reduced failed transactions from 7% to 0.5% in 48 hours
Problem
Customers experienced transient payment failures after a deploy. Volume of support tickets increased and some payments were double-submitted.
Constraints
Live system, limited engineering time, need to avoid customer billing issues and preserve data integrity.
Action
I reproduced the failure from logs, isolated a race condition in the payment retry logic, introduced a safe server-side guard, and coordinated a small rollback+patch with engineers. I communicated recommended workarounds to support and published short runbook steps.
Outcome
Failed payments dropped to 0.5% within 48 hours. No customers were double-charged. The runbook reduced average ticket handling time by 18% for that issue class.
Confusing onboarding flow — improved activation rate by 12% with small UX and docs changes
Problem
New users frequently dropped out during a multi-step setup; support tickets showed identical points of confusion.
Constraints
Product roadmap limited immediate UI changes; need low-effort, quick wins to help new users now.
Action
I mapped the funnel, captured common confusion points, added inline help text and a short checklist in the onboarding email, and worked with product to prioritize a small UI tweak in the next sprint.
Outcome
Activation (first successful task) rose 12% in the month after the change. Support tickets for the onboarding area dropped by half.
Recurring manual reporting — saved ~6 hours/week through lightweight automation
Problem
Operations ran a weekly report that required manual extraction, transformation, and emailing to stakeholders.
Constraints
No new vendor tools allowed; preference for a low-maintenance solution that engineers could review quickly.
Action
I created a small script that pulled sanitized data via existing APIs, generated the report, and delivered it to a shared location. I documented the setup and added a scheduled CI job to run it.
Outcome
Saved ~6 hours per week across the team. The report was reliable and auditable; stakeholders received it on time without manual steps.
